Tissue regeneration is the regrowth of a damaged or missing organ part from the remaining tissue. Ability to regenerate large parts of an organisms is related to complexity.

Lower developed the animal the better the regeneration capability

. Salamanders are capable of regenerating entire limbs. Facilitated by de-differentiation of mesenchymal cells (muscle, cartilage, bone) to blastemal cells. Cells in the blastema proliferate and re-differentiate to regenerate the limb.

Human regeneration capacity more limited. Certain tissues do have capacity to regenerate (e.g. liver, bone, connective tissue)

This dynamic process ensures that the intestinal epithelium is constantly replenished, maintaining the integrity of the barrier function and supporting efficient nutrient absorption. It also plays a crucial role in responding to injuries or infections, promoting rapid repair and recovery of the intestinal tissue.
